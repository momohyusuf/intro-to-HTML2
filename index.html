Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer widely considered one of the greatest players in the history of the sport. Born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, his full name is Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro. Ronaldo's career began at Sporting Lisbon in Portugal before he caught the eye of Manchester United, where he played from 2003 to 2009. During his time at Manchester United, he won three English Premier League titles and the UEFA Champions League in the 2007-2008 season. In 2008, he was awarded the FIFA World Player of the Year. In 2009, Ronaldo made a high-profile move to Real Madrid for what was then a world-record transfer fee. His time with Real Madrid was highly successful, and he became the club's all-time leading scorer. Ronaldo won numerous domestic and international titles with Real Madrid, including four Champions League titles. In 2018, Ronaldo made a surprising transfer to Juventus in Italy's Serie A. He continued to showcase his goal-scoring prowess and leadership on the field. Ronaldo has also been a key figure for the Portuguese national team, earning caps in multiple UEFA European Championships and FIFA World Cups. He helped Portugal win the UEFA Euro 2016 tournament and has been an integral part of their success. Ronaldo is known for his incredible goal-scoring ability, athleticism, versatility, and dedication to fitness. Off the field, he is involved in various charitable activities and has a significant presence on social media.
